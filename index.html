What exactly is "Carbon"?

In this context, "Carbon" refers to Carbon Dioxide (CO2) and other greenhouse gases (GHGs) like Methane. These gases trap heat in our atmosphere, acting like a blanket that warms the planet‚Äîa phenomenon known as the Greenhouse Effect.

Why must we remove it from the atmosphere?

Excess carbon from human activity (burning fossil fuels, deforestation) has tipped nature's balance. This causes:

  • Rising global temperatures (Global Warming).
  • Extreme weather events (droughts, floods).
  • Ocean acidification affecting marine life.

Removing it allows us to stay below the 1.5¬∞C warming limit set by the Paris Agreement.

üí∞ The Credit System

What is a Carbon Credit?

Think of a Carbon Credit as a verified certificate. One credit equals one metric ton of CO2 that has been either removed from the air (by trees/soil) or prevented from being emitted.

Companies buy these credits to "offset" their own unavoidable emissions.

How does Glocarbon verify my credits?

We use a 3-Step Verification Process aligned with VeraCarbon & Gold Standard:

  1. AI Satellite Scan: Measures biomass density from space.
  2. Drone/Ground Data: You upload specific site imagery.
  3. Audit: Our algorithm calculates the tonnage and issues a digital certificate.

What are the SDGs?

  • SDG 13 (Climate Action): Reducing CO2.
  • SDG 15 (Life on Land): Protecting forests and biodiversity.
  • SDG 1 (No Poverty): Paying farmers for ecosystem services.
